Our Team

Harini Venkatesh

Chapter Head

Harini is a senior from Brentwood, NH. She first fell in love with problem-solving through a program at her elementary school and became an avid competitor in math contests, and has participated in the USAJMO and Math Prize for Girls competitions, among others! After participating in an INTEGIRLS competition in 2020, she joined the community as a problem writer and has since worked to promote its mission to inspire math-loving female and nonbinary students. When she's not doing math, Harini enjoys singing along to Broadway soundtracks, reading fantasy and mystery novels, and watching Chicago Med.

Anika Sivarasa

Chapter Head

Anika is a senior from Atkinson, NH. She has been interested in math and science from an early age, first gaining exposure to competition math in elementary school. Over the years, she has participated in math competitions such as MATHCOUNTS and AIME. She has been part of INTEGIRLS since 2023, and wants to help minimize the gender gap in STEM. She is also very passionate about robotics, and enjoys dancing and listening to music in her free time.

Katie Ji

Chapter Head

Katie is a senior from Sudbury, MA. She has been pursuing math as a hobby since elementary school. Katie has participated in well known competitions such as AIME, HMMT, CMIMC, Purple Comet, and more. She first joined the INTEGIRLS team in 2024, and hopes to introduce competitive math to more non-binary and female contestants. Besides math and other sciences, Katie also has a strong passion for violin, drawing, and dancing.

Sophia Balaraman

Sophia is a sophomore from Exeter, NH who has a liking for all types of puzzles. She loves competing in math and has done many well known high school math competitions, such as the ARML, the The CMIMC, and the Girls in Math at Yale where she has obtained some individual and some team awards. She also competed in the AMC 10 and 12, where she qualified for AIME. In her free time, Sophia enjoys playing soccer, listening to music, coding, doing the New York Times puzzles, and she is an avid reader.

Vedika Soneji

Vedika Soneji is a senior at Westborough High in MA. She has qualified for AIME and Math Prize for Girls and has won the theme round at HMMT. She has also co-authored and published a paper on low-dimensional topology with an emphasis on projective geometry. Besides math, she does dance, badminton, and DECA.
